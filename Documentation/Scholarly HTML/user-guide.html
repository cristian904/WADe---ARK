Case study 1 (Exploring museums)

This case study shows how users can explore museums from all over Romania. The ARK interface presents a visualisation of the country with all museums spread across the map. A user can zoom in on the city they like and select which museum galery he wants to visit. He will click on the museum location and them view all the artwork from the gallery (Figure 1).

The user will be redirected to a new page where he can see different statistics on the selected museum and also all the works of art available. Available statistics:

  • Pie chart describing the state of the artworks: (good state, bad state, mediocre state etc.) (Figure 2)
  • Bar plot showing the artists present in the museum and how many works they have there. (Figure 3)
  • Bar plot describing what types of work is available in the museum and how many of them. (Figure 4)

Bellow these statistics you can see all the available artwork from the selected museum. Hovering over the image will give you some general information and then the user can click on it and get full details about the art work. (Figure 5)

On the art details page the user can find out general knowledge about the selected piece of art like author, year, museum in which you can see it, type of work, real size and a short description (Figure 6). Below you can see two lists of recommended art works (available through a carousel type scroll) witch shows art from the same author (Figure 6) and art from the same museum (Figure 6).
